## About my job

Currently, I work at the UH Bookstore computer department, aka Techub. I used to work in the customer service department, but after being 
there for a while, I switched over because I wanted experience working in a department relativey close to my area of study. At the Techub,
I sell computers and electronics, accessories, and software for departments. 

In our department, we do special orders for people when they want custom specs on a computer, as well as transferring merchandise, computer 
repairs, and keep records of our products. Because of this, me and my colleagues collaborate together in creating our own spreadsheets and 
forms to make recording information easier. We created our forms from scratch on programs like FileMaker and Numbers and they have 
functions that calculate formulas and upload data of merchandise when we need it. This lets us work as a team to stay organized and stay 
communicated.

## What I learned

The files we made at work taught me how to use spreadsheets and FileMaker, as well as how to stay organized with my online data. It was 
surprising to me at first because I didn't know about all these features beforehand and it really makes a difference in how efficiently we
work. These skills can be useful for the future in my career as a programmer and I'm glad I learned them early on.
