Ever since I started learning to code, I relied on online resources to help me solve my problems. Looking up forum threads like StackOverflow helped answer majority of my questions and if I couldn’t find my answer, I would rely on the professor or TA. But I never thought of creating a thread myself to solve a problem. It might be that I was intimidated to post about my ignorance online or that I was aware that there were guidelines set to posting on these forums. However, I wasn’t curious enough to learn about what it takes to post effective, smart questions on the internet. 

Eric Steven Raymond had a very detailed essay regarding the precepts of asking smart questions. It made me contemplate how professional hackers have to deal with questions everyday and I realize that it takes a certain amount of consideration for them if you’re stuck on a problem and want a constructive response back. Many people post on the forums as they’re so easily accessible, but some don’t always get the answer their looking for. Most of the time, only those who can appeal to the professionals can expect a positive response.
